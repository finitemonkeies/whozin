Refine the current Whozin brand book into a final, production-ready brand system that will directly inform implementation inside the product later, including app icon, logo usage, and in-app brand application.

Important context:
This is not just a presentation deck. The output will be handed off for actual product branding work inside the app. That means the identity system must be clear, consistent, implementable, and usable across:
- app icon
- splash / welcome surfaces
- in-app logo usage
- CTA treatments
- product marketing assets
- social cards
- future design/system decisions

The current version is the strongest so far. Keep the overall direction. This is a final refinement pass, not a reinvention.

What is working now:
- The deck feels more specific to Whozin
- The cover is stronger and more asymmetric
- The product-expression pages feel more real
- The social/marketing pages are more art-directed
- The W glyph is improved and moving in the right direction
- The overall brand feels private, social, nightlife-native, and premium

What still needs to be fixed:

1. Remove unsupported product/security claims
- Do not use technical claims like “end-to-end encrypted” unless they are explicitly verified
- Keep privacy language strong, but brand-safe
- Prefer:
  - Private by default
  - Just you and your people
  - No public check-ins
  - No broadcast feed
- Avoid introducing legal, security, or infrastructure claims that may not be true

2. Standardize the refined W everywhere
- The brand book must use one final W glyph system consistently across every logo appearance
- Do not mix old and new marks
- Update all lockups, header marks, app icon examples, and social asset examples to use the same refined W
- Treat this as the final canonical mark unless a stronger version is created in this round

3. Push the W one last level if needed
- The current W is better, but still may need one more level of craft
- Keep it bold, geometric, legible, premium, and scalable
- Make it feel unmistakably custom
- It should work as:
  - app icon
  - monochrome mark
  - small in-app mark
  - social avatar / favicon style mark
- Test it visually in:
  - gradient tile
  - white on dark
  - dark on light
  - very small sizes
- Avoid gimmicks, over-stylization, or hard-to-read forms
- The goal is memorable and ownable, not flashy

4. Remove any visual details that cheapen the premium tone
- If any pages use emoji or other overly casual symbols, replace them with more premium, intentional visual language
- Avoid anything that makes the deck feel like a mockup instead of a serious brand system
- Keep the tone elevated, culturally aware, and selective

5. Make the cover feel like a true brand statement
- The current cover is improved, but it should feel even more iconic
- It should be presentation-worthy and implementation-worthy
- It should feel like the moment the brand locks in
- Push composition, scale, hierarchy, atmosphere, and confidence
- Make it feel less like a slide and more like a signature opening frame

6. Make the system implementation-ready
Because this brand book will be used for actual app branding work, the final output should clearly define:
- canonical app icon
- canonical logo mark
- acceptable lockups
- monochrome uses
- minimum sizes
- clear space
- preferred gradients
- preferred colors for CTAs
- preferred in-app usage patterns
- what to use in small mobile contexts versus large marketing contexts

7. Keep the strongest current direction
Preserve:
- dark foundation
- hot pink / fuchsia / violet / electric purple core
- premium nightlife atmosphere
- privacy-forward positioning
- social-proof-led storytelling
- “The Move” as a premium/high-signal concept
- sharp, product-native copy like:
  - See who’s going before you go.
  - Know before you go.
  - Your crew is here.
  - 12 friends are going.
  - Private by default.

What to avoid:
- unsupported security claims
- mixed icon systems
- generic startup deck energy
- placeholder-looking mockups
- emoji-driven product examples
- repetitive fallback copy
- anything that feels crypto, gamer, EDM flyer, or Figma template
- anything beautiful but too vague to implement inside a real app
