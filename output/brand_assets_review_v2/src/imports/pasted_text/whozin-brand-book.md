Design a premium, high-conviction brand book for Whozin.

Whozin is not a generic event app. It is a private, nightlife-native social product that helps people see who’s going before they go. The brand should feel dark, magnetic, selective, social, and culturally aware. It should feel like the energy of going out at night, but controlled and high-signal, never chaotic, childish, or EDM-poster cheesy.

The current direction is close in mood but too generic. Make this new brand book feel unmistakably Whozin.

Core brand idea:
Whozin is the private social layer for nights out.
It helps you know where your people are, what the move is, and whether something is worth going to before you commit.

Strategic tensions to express:
- Private but electric
- Premium but accessible
- Social but not noisy
- Nightlife-native but not trashy
- Confident but not corporate
- Youthful but not immature

Creative direction:
- Dark interface foundation with real depth
- Black, smoked charcoal, ink, soft graphite surfaces
- Brand accents should center on hot pink, fuchsia, violet, and electric purple
- Accent colors should feel disciplined and signature, not rainbow-heavy
- Avoid broad generic palettes unless clearly labeled as support-only
- Use glow, blur, translucency, and atmospheric gradients sparingly and intentionally
- Overall feel should be “exclusive guest list meets modern social product”
- Think cinematic nightlife ambience, not gamer UI, not crypto, not festival flyer

Typography:
- Bold, compact, modern sans serif for headlines
- Clean, neutral sans serif for UI and body
- Headlines should feel sharp, confident, and slightly compressed if appropriate
- Tight tracking on hero headlines
- Wide tracking only for micro labels, pills, and status markers
- Avoid generic startup-presentations typography
- Typography should feel editorial and premium, not template-y

Copy tone:
- Short, social, confident, in-the-know
- Conversational, not corporate
- Never say generic lines like “the private social layer for nights out” over and over with no product specificity
- Use product-native language such as:
  - See who’s going.
  - Before you go.
  - Private by default.
  - The Move.
  - Your crew is here.
  - 4 friends are going.
  - Picking up tonight.
- Show the mechanic of Whozin through the copy, not just brand adjectives

What to include in the brand book:
1. Cover
- Strong, cinematic opening page
- Dark premium composition
- Hero statement that feels ownable and sharp
- Brand should feel immediately distinct

2. Brand foundation
- Mission
- Brand idea
- Audience feel / emotional promise
- Key brand tensions
- What Whozin is
- What Whozin is not
- Include do/don’t language that separates it from generic event marketplaces and generic social apps

3. Voice and messaging
- Core messaging pillars
- Tagline options
- Approved short-form copy
- CTA language
- Social proof language
- Privacy language
- Examples of good vs bad brand voice

4. Logo system
- Primary mark
- App icon
- Lockups
- Clear space
- Minimum size
- Monochrome versions
- Incorrect usage examples
- Present these in a polished, premium way, not as basic template blocks

5. Color system
- Primary brand colors
- Surface colors
- Text hierarchy
- Accent/support colors
- Usage ratios
- Glow/gradient rules
- Make it clear which colors are signature versus situational
- Avoid making blue/green/orange feel equally core if they are not

6. Typography system
- Display
- Headline
- Body
- Caption
- Micro label
- Show real brand copy, not placeholder lorem or generic labels

7. Visual language
- Shape language
- Corner radius logic
- Surface treatments
- Borders
- Shadows
- Blur and glow behavior
- Motion principles
- Image and art direction
- Define the atmosphere clearly: nightlife, social momentum, privacy, anticipation

8. Product expression
- Show how the brand appears inside the app
- Event cards
- Friend activity
- Social proof states
- “The Move” feature moments
- Privacy-forward onboarding or welcome moments
- These should feel like Whozin product storytelling, not random UI kit examples

9. Social and marketing assets
- Open Graph cards
- Instagram story frames
- Social post templates
- App Store creative direction
- Outdoor/OOH or flyer-inspired concept if useful
- Use real, believable Whozin scenarios such as:
  - “4 friends are going to Subtronics.”
  - “Your crew is here.”
  - “See who’s going before you go.”
- Show momentum and anticipation, not just static brand wallpaper

10. Dos and don’ts
- What makes it feel like Whozin
- What makes it feel generic, overdesigned, noisy, juvenile, or off-brand

Visual design expectations:
- The composition should feel intentional and art-directed
- Avoid “template brand deck” energy
- Avoid over-explaining with too many bland cards
- Fewer, stronger examples are better than lots of generic samples
- Make every page feel presentation-ready and founder-level
- The deck should feel premium enough to hand to investors, designers, creative partners, and agencies

Avoid:
- Generic SaaS deck layouts
- Flat black backgrounds with random gradients slapped on
- Overuse of blue/green/orange as co-equal brand colors
- Empty mood words without application
- UI-kit filler content like “Primary CTA / Secondary / Tertiary” unless deeply customized to the brand
- Repetitive generic copy like “the private social layer for nights out” with no specificity
- Anything that feels like crypto, esports, club flyer spam, or a Figma community template

Output:
Create a cohesive, high-end brand book that feels sharper, more specific, and more culturally tuned than a standard startup identity deck. The result should feel unmistakably Whozin: private, social, nightlife-native, premium, and full of anticipation.
