# Whozin Marketing Site Style Guide

## Objective

This guide translates the in-app brand into a public-facing marketing site without losing the product's private, nightlife-native feel.

Primary marketing goal:

- convert curiosity into account creation

Secondary marketing goals:

- explain the product in one screenful
- make social proof tangible
- reduce privacy concerns early

## Core Marketing Message

Primary headline territory:

- See who's going before you go.
- Find your people before the night starts.
- Know the move.

Primary supporting message:

- Whozin helps you see which friends are going, spot momentum, and decide faster.

Privacy reassurance line:

- Private by default. No public profiles. No location tracking.

## Page Structure

### 1. Hero

Purpose:

- make the value obvious in under 5 seconds

Include:

- bold headline
- short support sentence
- primary CTA
- secondary CTA
- ambient social proof visual
- privacy reassurance chip

Recommended pattern:

- left or centered headline
- oversized display typography
- blurred gradient atmosphere
- avatar stack or event card preview

### 2. Social Proof Section

Purpose:

- show why Whozin is different

Include:

- friend stacks
- "4 friends are going" examples
- momentum badges
- light narrative around confidence and timing

### 3. How It Works

Recommended three-step structure:

1. Connect or sign in
2. See who is going
3. Decide fast

Each step should be short, visual, and verb-led.

### 4. Trust And Privacy

Purpose:

- handle emotional objections before signup

Include:

- private-by-default language
- visibility control
- no public profile positioning
- optional no-location-tracking statement if still true operationally

### 5. Final CTA

Purpose:

- close with one strong action

Recommended CTA:

- Join to See the List
- Get Started
- See Who's Going

## Art Direction

### Visual Tone

- dark background
- pink and purple energy accents
- selective blue support glow
- smoked-glass surfaces
- premium nightlife poster feel

### Background Treatment

Use:

- radial glows
- soft blur orbs
- subtle gradients
- sparse grid or texture if needed

Avoid:

- flat white sections as the dominant look
- busy collage layouts
- bright rainbow gradients
- loud neon overload

## Typography

### Headline Style

- bold
- tight tracking
- high contrast
- short phrases

Recommended sizing behavior:

- desktop hero: very large display scale
- mobile hero: still bold and immediate, 2 to 3 lines max

### Body Style

- clean sans
- medium or regular weight
- muted zinc tone
- short paragraphs

### Eyebrows And Labels

- uppercase
- wide letter spacing
- compact size
- used sparingly

## Color Usage

### Primary Palette

- black: `#000000`
- near-black zinc: `#18181b`
- zinc-800: `#27272a`
- white: `#ffffff`
- hot pink: `#db2777`
- electric purple: `#9333ea`

### Secondary Palette

- signal blue: `#3b82f6`
- violet: `#a855f7`
- success green: `#22c55e`
- muted zinc text: `#71717a`

### Color Rules

- keep the page mostly dark
- use the pink-purple gradient for primary conversion actions
- use blue as a secondary highlight, not the main brand
- use green only for success or confirmed state language

## Components

### Primary Button

Use for:

- hero CTA
- final CTA
- signup conversion

Style:

- pink to purple gradient
- white text
- pill or 2xl radius
- soft shadow

### Secondary Button

Use for:

- sign in
- learn more
- alternate conversion path

Style:

- dark surface
- thin border
- white text

### Social Proof Card

Include:

- event title
- friend count
- small avatar row
- date/location
- subtle gradient or glow

### Privacy Chip

Style:

- low-contrast zinc pill
- small icon
- concise trust statement

## Motion

Marketing motion should feel controlled and premium.

Use:

- fade up on hero copy
- soft scale-in on key visuals
- slight hover lift on cards
- glow shift on CTA hover

Avoid:

- bounce
- exaggerated parallax
- flashy looping motion

## Imagery

Preferred imagery:

- dark concert and nightlife moments
- silhouettes, crowd energy, venue lighting
- event poster compositions
- blurred avatars or identity-light social visuals

Avoid:

- obvious stock-photo smiling office groups
- brightly lit daytime festival imagery as the main direction
- literal maps, dashboards, or generic SaaS graphics

## Copy System

### Good Copy Examples

- See who's going.
- Your circle, before the RSVP.
- Find your people before the night starts.
- Private by default.
- Know the move.

### Bad Copy Examples

- Discover unforgettable experiences with your community.
- The all-in-one platform for event engagement.
- Maximize your nightlife potential.

## Layout Rules

- keep each section focused on one message
- use generous vertical spacing
- let headlines carry the page
- keep support text short
- show the product early

## Responsive Rules

- prioritize headline clarity over decorative visuals on mobile
- move ambient visuals behind content, not beside it, on narrow screens
- keep CTAs visible without long scrolls
- maintain strong contrast across devices

## Recommended Site Formula

For a simple launch page:

1. Hero with social-proof headline
2. Product preview strip
3. Three-step explanation
4. Privacy/trust block
5. Final CTA

## Build Principle

The site should feel like an invitation into a private signal network, not a brochure for an event app.
